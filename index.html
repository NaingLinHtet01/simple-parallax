Bagan


A Glimpse into an Ancient Kingdom

Introduction


Bagan, located in the Mandalay Region of Myanmar, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's most significant archaeological areas. Once the capital of the Pagan Kingdom from the 9th to the 13th centuries, Bagan is home to over 2,000 temples, stupas, and pagodas spread across a vast plain by the Ayeyarwady (Irrawaddy) River.

Historical Background


Bagan's history dates back to the 2nd century AD, but its prominence rose significantly between the 9th and 13th centuries when it became the heart of the Pagan Kingdom. This era marked a golden age for Burmese culture, religion, and architecture. The kingdom's rulers, including King Anawrahta, who is credited with founding the Bagan Empire in 1044, constructed numerous temples and monuments, many of which still stand today.

Modern-Day Bagan


Today, Bagan is a major tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the globe who come to marvel at its historical and architectural wonders. The best way to experience Bagan's vast landscape is by hot air balloon, which offers breathtaking views of the temples at sunrise or sunset. Additionally, exploring the area by bike or horse cart provides a closer look at the intricate details of the temples and the daily life of the local people.
